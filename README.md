
# Telegram as a Transformative Criminal Marketplace

## Authors
Manik Kaur, Nagasai Anjani Kumar Thadishetty, Anu G Bourgeois, David Maimon

## Abstract

Purpose: Messaging platforms like Telegram have become key marketplaces for stolen personally identifiable information (PII), transforming how such data is organized, distributed, and monetized. Guided by Routine Activity Theory, we explore: (1) how Telegram’s architecture enables the convergence of offenders, targets, and weak guardianship; (2) which types of data are most frequently shared; and (3) how victimization patterns vary by geography and institution.This research addresses a critical gap in understanding how surface -accessible messaging platforms have transformed identity theft operations, moving beyond darknet-focused cybercrime studies.

Methods: We developed a multistage framework to explore Telegram’s role in identity theft. Channels were identified via keyword searches and network exploration, followed by automated data collection. Extracted posts were processed to convert unstructured content into usable intelligence. This enabled tracking of data flows across 25 Telegram channels throughout 2023, offering visibility into underground markets typically hidden from traditional investigations.

Results: Credit card data dominated, comprising 82% of all shared PII, highlighting strong financial incentives. Geographic analysis showed unexpected concentrations in Texas, California, and Florida. Temporal spikes in March and June aligned with consumer spending peaks, suggesting strategic timing by offenders.

Conclusion: These findings update Routine Activity Theory by demonstrating how Telegram creates a unique ”criminal convergence space” where motivated offenders, suitable targets, and absent guardians interact. Unlike darknet markets, Telegram’s low barrier to entry and anonymity features facilitate identity theft while impeding enforcement. This study reveals new cybercrime dynamics and supports future development of real-time monitoring, victim alert systems, and predictive risk models.

Keywords: Telegram, Instant Messaging Platforms, PII, Identity Theft, Financial Fraud, Cybercrime.
